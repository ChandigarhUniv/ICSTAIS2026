üìå Call for Posters

ICSTAIS 2026 will include a dedicated Poster Session to provide a forum for presenting and discussing work in progress, ongoing and upcoming research challenges, new research directions, and novel approaches in the field of applied cryptography and cybersecurity.

We especially value interdisciplinary work and collaborative projects between academia and industry. High-quality poster presentations are considered an integral part of ICSTAIS, and a Best Poster Award will be presented.

üìù Submission Guidelines

  • Authors are invited to submit a short proposal (5-7 pages). Template is availabe in Instruction for Author's Page: click to redirect to Instruction for author page
  • Proposals must include: title, abstract, motivation, contributions, and (if available) preliminary results.
  • The proposal should clearly state the difference from prior work or ongoing submissions.
  • Proposals will be evaluated on their potential to spark discussion, exchange of ideas, and collaborations.

  • Proposals are not blinded and must include author names, affiliations, and contact details.
  • A single corresponding author must be designated.
  • At least one author per accepted poster must register and present at the conference.

Accepted posters will be displayed on tripods provided by the organizers. Poster instructions will be sent upon acceptance. All accepted posters will be published in the post-proceedings of ICSTAIS.
