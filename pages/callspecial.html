Call for Special Sessions

Proposals are solicited for Special Sessions to be held in conjunction with ICSTAIS2026. Each ICSTAIS2026 workshop provides a forum to address a specific topic at the forefront of cryptography, Artificial Intelligence, privacy, and cybersecurity research. All Special Sessions must be held physically during the conference and presentations are expected to be delivered in person. At least one of the authors of each accepted Special Session paper would be expected to register at an author rate for the main conference. Each accepted Special Session will receive one complementary registration. All Special Sessions are expected to have a competitive Call for Papers and organize their own selection process. Post-proceedings of all Special Sessions will be published in Springer‚Äôs SIST series. An ICSTAIS2026 workshop best paper award will be selected from the accepted papers of all Special Sessions.

Deadline: Submissions of Special Session proposals must reach the Special Session Chair by September 21, 2025, AoE.

Special Session Proposal Submission Guidelines

  • Special Session title and acronym
  • A draft ‚ÄúCall for Papers‚Äù articulating the scope and topics covered by the Special Session
  • A brief summary and justification for the Special Session, including short feedback from any previous editions on acceptance rates, number of participants, etc.
  • Expected number of submissions and acceptance rate (should be 30% or less)
  • Expected number of attendees [Minimum of 5 papers to be presented in Special Session]
  • Names of Special Session chair and Co-Chair, list of program committee
  • Separate paper submission portal link for call for special session
  • One-paragraph biographical sketch for each Special Session chair (qualifications, research, and organizing experience)
  • Proposals should be submitted as a single PDF file. Email to icstais@cumail.in

Proposal Evaluation Criteria

  • The Special Session is within the scope of cybersecurity and concerns an emerging and important technical topic of high interest in the community
  • Its novelty with respect to other forums
  • Its likely impact on the target community, including potential high participation
  • Research caliber of the Special Session PC committee and PC chairs

Note: All Submitted Special Session Papers along with accepted papers & paper registration proofs must be emailed to icstais@cumail.in before January 15, 2026.
