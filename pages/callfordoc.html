Call for Doctoral Symposium

The ICSTAIS2026 Doctoral Symposium invites Research Scholars (Masters/ Ph.D.) in the field of Cybersecurity, Artificial Intelligence, and related areas to submit their ongoing or planned dissertation research for discussion and feedback. This event provides an excellent platform for research scholars to present their research, engage with established experts, and receive constructive input to advance their thesis work.

The Doctoral Symposium not only offers early career researchers a chance to gain experience in presenting to an international audience but also to receive invaluable feedback about their work from members of the ICSTAIS community.

Objectives

  • Offer research scholars independent, constructive feedback on their research topics, approaches, and progress.
  • Build a community of emerging scholars in Cybersecurity and AI.
  • Promote networking and exchange between students and senior researchers in the ICSTAIS community.

Who Should Participate

  • Research Scholars at any stage of their PhD program working on topics relevant to the scope of ICSTAIS2026.
  • Early- and mid-stage students especially encouraged to apply, whether problem definition, research plan, or preliminary results are being developed.
  • Although this forum is primarily targeted toward Ph.D. students, BE (Undergraduate) and ME (Postgraduate) students who have formulated a research project and obtained some good results will also be considered and encouraged to apply.

Submission Guidelines

  • The abstract should align with ICSTAIS2026 theme.
  • Extended Abstract: (5â€“7 pages, including figures and references). Only single-author submissions are permitted, with the name of the PhD supervisor(s) included below that of the author. Structure must include:
    • Overall problem and motivation: Explain why this research is important and identify the key research question(s) and challenges.
    • Objectives and contribution: Present goals of your research, why your work is novel, and main contributions.
    • Methods and results: Present scientific methods, suitability, preliminary or expected results, and current status.
    • Future work and research plan: Summarise intended future work, milestones, and evaluation plan.
  • The submitted paper will be evaluated on:
    • Originality and novelty of the research
    • Relevance to ICSTAIS
    • Quality, clarity, precision of problem statement, and reflection
  • Curriculum Vitae: A short CV highlighting academic background and research interests.
  • Letter of Recommendation: (optional) from thesis advisor endorsing participation (recommended for early-stage students).
  • All the above must be merged in a single document and uploaded on Microsoft CMT.

Format

  • Oral presentation during the symposium.
  • Senior researchers will provide detailed feedback.
  • Registered & orally presented abstracts may be published in post-conference proceedings.
